Wed24
Arrival · settle in

Touchdown & the first swim

Boat in at 1pm → car → Sant'Angelo check-in → Maronti
+

📍 The flow

Arrive 13:00, collect the rental car at the port, and drive ~25–30 min south to Sant'Angelo. The village itself is car-free — you park up top and walk down, so travel light for the check-in and come back for the rest.

Once you've dropped the bags, ease in with the closest, easiest beach on the island: Maronti, a long ribbon of dark volcanic sand a few minutes below the village. First float in the Tyrrhenian, then back up to Sant'Angelo for a sunset spritz on the little harbour square.

📍 Sant'Angelo 📍 Maronti Beach 📍 Ischia Porto (car pickup)
⚓ A little history

Sant'Angelo grew up as a fishing hamlet under its dramatic Torre — the rocky islet tied to the village by a thin sand isthmus. No cars have ever really fit down there, which is exactly why it stayed so pretty while the rest of the island modernised.

💡 Local tip

Maronti's eastern end, near the Fumarole, has natural volcanic steam vents — the sand is genuinely hot to the touch, and a few spots steam at low tide. Don't dig deep with bare feet there.

🍴 Lunch on the sand

Right on Maronti, a few steps from the towel. Lido Sammy is a raised wooden deck over the sand with a sweep of view back to Sant'Angelo; Bagno Antonio (a Talented Mr. Ripley filming spot) brings homemade plates to your sunbed. A panino and a cold beer is perfectly in keeping — and Maronti runs cheaper than the rest of the island.

📍 Lido Sammy 📍 Bagno Antonio

⛴️ Getting there

Direct seasonal ferries run Ischia → Capri in roughly 50 minutes (first boats ~9am, returns in the afternoon; ~€27+ each way). It's a popular route in summer, so book online a few days ahead and re-check the exact times the day before — schedules shift. A weekday like this is deliberately calmer than the weekend crush.

📍 Marina Grande, Capri ⛴️ Ferry times & tickets

🌅 A relaxed Capri, our way

Skip the march-everywhere checklist. A lovely food-and-sea day: a small-boat tour around the island (the Faraglioni stacks, sea caves, a swim stop — and the Blue Grotto if the sea is flat and the queue sane), then up to the Piazzetta for the people-watching, the Gardens of Augustus for the Faraglioni view, and a long lunch. If there's appetite, the chairlift up to Monte Solaro from Anacapri is the island's best panorama.

🏛️ A little history

Emperor Tiberius ran the entire Roman Empire from Capri for his last decade, from the cliff-top Villa Jovis. The island has been a bolt-hole for emperors, poets and film stars ever since — the Faraglioni rocks even have their own resident blue lizard found nowhere else on Earth.

💡 Local tip

The Blue Grotto only works in calm seas and closes in any swell — treat it as a bonus, not a plan. And eat away from the Piazzetta: walk five minutes down any side lane and prices halve.

⛵ Private boat · Procida + around Ischia

Your full-day private charter (booking in your hands). A dream itinerary if they'll do it: slip out of Sant'Angelo, swim at Sorgeto and the cliffs of the south-west, round the island past Forio, then cross to tiny Procida — the most cinematic of the three islands — for Marina Corricella and a swim at Pozzo Vecchio (the "Il Postino" beach). Lunch aboard or in Corricella.

📍 Marina Corricella 📍 Pozzo Vecchio beach 📍 Sorgeto (swim stop)
🎬 A little history

Procida was Italy's Capital of Culture in 2022 — pastel fishermen's houses stacked over Corricella harbour, painted bright so sailors could spot their own home from the sea. It starred in Il Postino and The Talented Mr. Ripley, yet stays gloriously untouristy.

💡 Local tip

Confirm with the skipper whether lunch is aboard or ashore, and whether snorkel gear is included — Procida's coves and the Ischia south coast are lovely for an easy surface snorkel.

Fri26
Beach · wine · sunset

West coast & the golden church

Citara or Cava dell'Isola · optional tasting · Soccorso at sundown
+

🏖️ The beach

Pick by mood. Citara is a broad sandy bay facing due west — easy, full-service, made for a slow beach day that rolls into the sunset. Cava dell'Isola is the wilder, free, cliff-backed cove a little north — younger crowd, fewer frills, gorgeous water.

📍 Citara Beach 📍 Cava dell'Isola

🍷 Optional: a wine stop

If you're up for it, the terraced vines of Pietratorcia (between Panza and Forio) do tastings of Ischia DOC whites grown on volcanic soil — a relaxed late-afternoon detour on the way back up. Entirely optional; only if the day wants it.

📍 Pietratorcia winery

🌇 Sunset

End at the Chiesa del Soccorso in Forio — a tiny whitewashed church on a spur over the sea, and the island's classic sunset spot. Be on its terrace as the sun drops; the whole village glows after.

📍 Chiesa del Soccorso
⚓ A little history

The Soccorso is a sailors' votive church — its walls are lined with maiolica tiles and ex-voto offerings from fishermen who made it home. The name itself means "the rescue."

🍴 Lunch on the sand

At Citara: La Citarea sits right on one of the bay's free beaches — panoramic terrace, fresh fish, feet-in-the-sand lunch. At wilder Cava dell'Isola there's just a simple beach kiosk for a panino and a granita, which is honestly all you need on a cove day.

📍 La Citarea 📍 Cava dell'Isola kiosk

Sat27
Beach · the turquoise day

San Montano & Lacco Ameno

The calmest bay on the island · stroll & apero when you've had enough sun
+

🏖️ The beach

Baia di San Montano (Lacco Ameno) is the most sheltered, most turquoise bay on Ischia — a horseshoe of calm, shallow, clear water that's perfect for nothing-but-floating. Sandy bottom, gentle entry, easy.

📍 Baia di San Montano

🚶 When you're sun-tired

Wander into Lacco Ameno, the island's most elegant little town — pastel facades, a smart seafront, and Il Fungo, the mushroom-shaped tuff rock sitting just offshore. Aperitivo on the water as the afternoon cools.

📍 Il Fungo 📍 Lacco Ameno centre
🏛️ A little history

This is where Western civilisation's Greek story begins: nearby Monte di Vico was Pithekoussai, one of the earliest Greek colonies in the West (8th c. BC). The famous "Nestor's Cup," carrying one of the oldest known Greek inscriptions, was dug up here — it's in the Villa Arbusto museum just up the hill. In the 1950s, publisher Angelo Rizzoli turned the town into a jet-set spa resort.

🍴 Lunch on the sand

The bay's own beach restaurant (Stabilimento S. Montano) has a self-service bar and a fish-focused kitchen right on the sand — no need to leave the turquoise. Or pack a panino; this is a bay made for lingering all afternoon.

📍 Lido San Montano
🍤
Dinner: Lacco Ameno has lovely seafood tables — or drive back south to shower & change. To lock together.
Sun28
Beach · first-ever snorkel

Cartaromana & the sunken city

Swim under the castle · float over Roman ruins
+

🏖️ The beach

Cartaromana sits right beneath the Castello Aragonese — arguably the most beautiful view-from-a-towel on the island. Calm, shallow, sheltered water and a couple of relaxed beach-bar tables. You get the postcard castle without ever booking a "tour."

📍 Cartaromana Beach 📍 Castello Aragonese

🤿 Snorkelling — total beginners welcome

This is the friendliest place imaginable for a first mask. The water is calm and shallow, and the Roman ruins of Aenaria sit at ~6m, so you see them clearly while just floating on the surface — no diving, no skill, you can wear a vest and never put your head under if you don't want to. Practise breathing through the tube where you can still stand, then drift out.

Best first-timer option: a guided snorkel over Aenaria (~40 min, ~€40–45pp, booking required) — mask & fins provided, a guide beside you, and they show the natural CO₂ "vents" fizzing up beneath the castle. Prefer to stay dry? A glass-bottom boat (~70 min, ~€30pp) shows the same ruins through the hull.

ℹ️ About Aenaria 🤿 Snorkel tour
🏛️ A little history

Aenaria was a Roman port lost to volcanic subsidence and forgotten for almost 2,000 years — rediscovered only in the 1970s and properly excavated in the last decade. Above it, the Castello Aragonese has been fortified since 474 BC; its causeway was built by Alfonso of Aragon in 1441.

💡 Local tip

Book the guided snorkel ahead — reservations are required and morning slots go first. Bring water shoes; the entry has a few stones.

🍴 Lunch on the sand

Cocò Mare is the dream here — directly on Cartaromana bay, spaghetti alle vongole with the Castello filling the view (reachable on foot, or by the little boat from Ischia Ponte). Prefer to stay light before the snorkel? A casual beach-bar panino does the job just as well.

📍 Cocò Mare
🏰
Dinner: Ischia Ponte is gorgeous after dark with the lit castle — or back to base to freshen up. To lock together.
Mon29
Home day · fixed

Sant'Angelo, farm lunch & hot springs

Solo morning walk · Fattoria Greca · Sorgeto dip · maybe Maronti
+

🌅 Morning

Stay around Sant'Angelo (work meeting in play). A solo coastal walk while the call happens, an espresso on the square, an easy beach hour.

🍽️ Lunch · Antica Fattoria Greca

Up onto the Punta Chiarito headland for farm-to-table lunch at the Antica Fattoria Greca (Az. Agricola Ruffano) — their own produce, big views over the sea, exactly the slow island meal this day is built around.

📍 Antica Fattoria Greca

♨️ Sorgeto Bay

The farm sits directly above Sorgeto — a cove where natural hot springs bubble straight into the sea, mixing with the waves into warm rock pools. A short cooling dip is the move (it's hot, rocky, and umbrella-free), then onward.

📍 Baia di Sorgeto

🏖️ Then

If it's too hot to linger, drift back toward Sant'Angelo and a small beach, or to Maronti for the late-afternoon shade — decide on the day.

💡 Local tip

Sorgeto has no shade and no umbrellas, and the springs are genuinely hot. Bring water shoes (rocky entry), go for a short swim rather than a sunbathe, and take water. You can also reach it by taxi-boat from Sant'Angelo (~€7pp) to skip the ~200 steps in the heat.

🏺 A little history

Punta Chiarito hides a Bronze/Iron Age settlement — and Ischia's whole spa culture rests on the same volcanic plumbing that heats Sorgeto. People have been bathing in these waters since antiquity.

Good to know

🚗 The car

Great for reaching beaches, but Sant'Angelo is car-free at the bottom — park up top and walk down. Roads are narrow and switchbacked; drive slow and don't rush the coastal bends.
